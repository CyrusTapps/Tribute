Frank "Pistol Pete" Eaton

An Oklahoma State University Legend

Here are some highlights from Frank's life and influence

  • October 26, 1860 - Born in Hartford, CT
  • Age 8 - His father, an abolitionist, was shot in cold blood by 6 former confederates
  • Age 15 - Won marksmanship and quickdraw competitions at Ft Gibson, OK
  • Ages 15-17 - Avenged his father's death by killing all 6 men responsible
  • Age 17 - Became a Deputy U.S. Marshall under Judge Isaac C. Parker, "the hanging judge"
  • Age 29 - After participating in the Oklahoma land rush, settled near Perkins, serving as sheriff and later as a blacksmith
  • 1956 - Selected as the inspiration for the mascot of Oklahoma A&M (now Oklahoma State University) and made a lifetime, honorary member of the alumni association
  • April 8, 1958- Died in Perkins, OK

“Frank Eaton, the law man, the grandfather, the blacksmith ... he’s this guy that we’re portraying. This is representing a man who is part of the history of Oklahoma.” - M. Willis, "Pistol Pete" mascot
